About UsThe African Centre for Security and Strategic Studies .

Mandate

Act as a non-state vehicle that addresses comprehensively current and future national, regional and continental security, safety, geopolitical and Defence matters of strategic interest.

Mandate

Promote national, regional and international safety and security related issues through Knowledge and Evidence based Policy and Legislative lobbying and advocacy

Undertake research works, provide advisories to state and non-state actors and influence policy and decision-making communities in the region on aspects of security and strategic matters

Build capacities of security challenged communities in the country, to enable them build sustainable coping mechanisms

Identify and map out risks and challenges to national, regional and continental security, safety and military affairs

Learn More

Mission

M

AFCESSS exists to promote through studies, research, policy advocacy, capacity building, situational analysis, dialogues, awareness creation, civic education and programmes, a sustainably secure and safe Africa for all, characterized by democratic and accountable leadership, democratic and accountable policing and citizen-focused security and military mechanisms.

Vision

V

To have a safe, secure and stable Eastern Africa and continental Africa for sustainable development, enhancement of democracy and dignity for all

History

H

The African Centre for Security and Strategic Studies (AFCESSS) is an autonomous, self-governing organisation established in 2010 with the aim of implementing programmes, spearheading research, capacity building and policy advocacy and legislative lobbying on governance and security sector reforms.
