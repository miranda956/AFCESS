Welcome to AFCESSS

VISION

To have a safe, secure and stable Eastern Africa and continental Africa for sustainable development, enhancement of democracy and dignity for all

Mission

AFCESSS exists to promote through studies, research, policy advocacy, capacity building, situational analysis, dialogues, awareness creation, civic education and programmes, a sustainably secure and safe Africa for all, characterized by democratic and accountable leadership, democratic and accountable policing and citizen-focused security and military mechanisms.

Core Values

Integrity: AFCESSS embraces transparency and accountability in all its operations. It subjects its work to the scrutiny of all through transparent procedures of planning and reporting of its activities

Compassion: AFCESSS demonstrates high levels of empathy for the poor, marginalized and vulnerable in target communities and countries facing extreme security challenges. AFCESSS recognizes these categories as being most vulnerable to negative effects of insecurity

Diligence: AFCESSS ensures that its procedures are pursued faithfully by the Governing structures. Its operations are underpinned by hard work and sacrifice among its staff.

Rule of Law: AFCESSS promotes respect for the law of the land in each state and the relevant international laws governing issues relevant to its mandate

Human rights: in conducting its work, AFCESSS safeguards and promotes the rights of its target groups; it pursues a policy that prohibits discrimination on any grounds whatsoever.

Gender equality and equity: AFCESSS recognizes different capacities and opportunities available to men and women, the youth and children as well as the historical, social and culturally constructed inequalities and insecurities existing amongst the people and the societies in general.

Our history

The African Centre for Security and Strategic Studies (AFCESSS) is an autonomous, self-governing organisation established in 2010 with the aim of implementing programmes, spearheading research, capacity building and policy advocacy and legislative lobbying on governance and security sector reforms.

The Leadership of AFCESSS has participated in security sector reforms from the beginning and has consistently been a part of the reform agenda to date. The Director was a member of the police vetting board, a member of the National Task Force on community policing and has participated in various government informed workshops on countering extreme violence in the country

Our initiatives are organized to feed into four broad programmatic themes namely, Stakeholder Dialogues and Engagements; Research, Policy Development and Legislative Lobbying; Advocacy, Networking and Outreach; and Capacity Building, Training and Knowledge Transfers.
